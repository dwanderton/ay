Things to do

We love Vermont, we hope you will do too!

If you are spending a bit of extra time in Vermont, there is plenty to do!! Please find below a list of just a few ideas!!

Ben & Jerry’s

1.5 miles from the Church | 3 minutes drive

Learn how Ben & Jerry’s gets so much deliciousness into every scoop! Take their 30-minute guided factory tour and learn how they make ice cream, and the quite striking move to a corporate feel after there purchase by Unilever. A 'cool' activity that me and Alice have enjoyed many times. More details here or view on Google Maps.

Lake Champlain

28.1 miles from the Church | 33 minutes drive

The lake was named after the French explorer Samuel de Champlain, who encountered it in July 1609. While the ports of Burlington, Vermont, Port Henry, New York, and Plattsburgh, New York today are primarily used by small craft, ferries, and lake cruise ships, they were of substantial commercial and military importance in the 18th and 19th centuries. We loved taking the ferry across from Burlington into Port Kent, New York - A beautiful and relaxing journey. More details here or view on Google Maps.

Icelandic Horse Farm

11.3 miles from the Church | 18 minutes drive

The Vermont Icelandic Horse Farm is near to Waitsfield and the Skinner Barn (the location of our Reception). They offer full-day and half-day trail rides. Rides and treks are by advance reservation, but everyone is welcome to visit the farm any time during the day and see the extraordinary horses. Iceland is of significance to me and Alice as it is a mid-point between Boston and London - where we have both changed flights and enjoyed the country while flying on the unusually named 'WOW Airlines'. More details here or view on Google Maps.

Big Eddy Covered Bridge

12.7 miles from the Church | 20 minutes drive

The covered bridges of Vermont are among its most cherished and symbolic historic resources. Two remain in the Village of Waitsfield, the Great Eddy and the Pine Brook. Both reflect the widespread construction of covered bridges on Vermont's public highways from around 1820 to 1904. Once me and Alice were in a shop and saw our first postcard of a covered bridge. We put it into our GPS and drove to find it - we didnt expect it to be over 2 hours away, but it was beautiful! More details here or view on Google Maps.

Madisonian Museum of Industrial Design

12.7 miles from the Church | 20 minutes drive

Open 12pm - 4pm on Friday, Saturday and Sunday the Mueseum is right next door to the Big Eddy Covered Bridge in Waitsfield! The mueseum represents a lifetime of appreciation for and devotion to good design, displaying the best designed and most artistic manufactured objects. From cars to toasters, and from toys to canoes this eccentric and eclectic collection will provide a brief relief from the gorgeous natural surroundings. More details here or view on Google Maps.

The Alchemist Brewery

11.8 miles from the Church | 19 minutes drive

Open Tueday to Saturday 11am to 7pm The Alchemist is a family run brewery specializing in fresh, unfiltered IPA. John and Jen Kimmich originally opened The Alchemist as a 60 seat brew pub in the village of Waterbury in 2003. After eight years of success and growing popularity, they decided to open a small production brewery. Today The Alchemist currently operates two breweries in Vermont. Based in Stowe Vermont, the town where David placed in a pie eating contest on Independence Day! More details here or view on Google Maps.

Vermont’s Highest Peak

17.2 miles from the Church | 29 minutes drive

Stowe Mountain Resort offers views and adventures that can’t be beat. Hike, drive or ride the gondola to the top of the mountain, then ride the new zipline down. Afterwards, drive route 108 through Smuggler’s Notch—possibly the most scenic stretch of road in Vermont. Its a beautiful drive we have taken that will also bring you close to the Von Trapp Family Lodge. More details here or view on Google Maps.

Von Trapp Family Lodge

12.2 miles from the Church | 21 minutes drive

A Little taste of Austria in Vermont. Situated on 2,500 acres in Stowe, Vermont, Trapp Family Lodge is a mountain resort featuring Austrian-inspired architecture and European-style accommodations. Alice and I saw our first fireflies in the field opposite the Trapp Deli and Bakery just a few minutes down the road, the fields were full of them - we reccommend to head here at dusk to catch a glimpse yourself. More details here or view on Google Maps.

World’s Tallest Filing Cabinet

27.1 miles from the Church | 28 minutes drive

Before hopping on the ferry towards New York State marvel at the prospect and reality of the World's Tallest Filing Cabinet. Located in what is described by pilgrims as a field of weeds, the World's Tallest Filing Cabinet may be an unexpected addition to your itinerary. While driving through Casey, Illinois, we hit up the World's Largest Rocking Chair, Wind Chimes and Golf Tee. Not to mention our drive from Boston to Strawberry Point, Iowa, to appreciate the World's Largest Strawberry. Visit and be a part of a soon to be forgottern history. More details here or view on Google Maps.

Morse Farm Maple Sugarworks

16.2 miles from the Church | 21 minutes drive

Morse Farm offers free sugar house tours and tasting, a nature trail, country store, and an outdoor Vermont farm life museum. After spending a few months we realised that no breakfast would ever feel complete again without a latering of Maple Syrup - especially if pancakes were involved. More details here or view on Google Maps.

ECHO Lake Aquarium and Science Center

28.2 miles from the Church | 35 minutes drive

This is one for those who are young, young at heart, or responsible for those who fit into categories prior. ECHO's mission is to educate and delight people about the ecology of the Lake Champlain Basin. One of Alice and my favorite things to do each time I return to Boston is to visit the New England Aquarium there is something indescribably beautiful about water and all it gives life to.More details here or view on Google Maps.

Lake Champlain Chocolates

27.2 miles from the Church | 28 minutes drive

Perhaps now one of the brands most recognised in Vermont, Lake Champlain Chocolates started off as a dare between a restaurant owner and a Pastry Chef who complained that the chocolates given to staff at Christmas were not up to scratch! After whipping up a batch it was decided the restaurant should be sold - the company was born. We both admit that these are some scrumptious confections and we look forward to sampling them again soon! More details here or view on Google Maps.
