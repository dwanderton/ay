The Church.

The Waterbury Congregational Church is at the center of the town of Waterbury, Vermont - which was settled over 250 years ago. Uniquely, the church is actually run by the Congregation itself. Attendees choose the minister, write the by-laws, hire the staff and run the show. The Church’s current pastor is Reverend Peter Plagge, who will be kindly administering our wedding ceremony!

 

 

Skinner Barn.

Built in 1891 and originally used as a dairy barn, Skinner Barn sits up on the eastern ridge of the Mad River Valley. The barn and farmhouse are the only remaining historical buildings of significance left in the area.

Q: Where should we stay?
A: A few resources to find a great place to stay in Waitsfield, Vermont: Airbnb.com and HomeAway.com. Skinner Barn also recommends using the Mad River Valley Chamber of Commerce to find lodging information and dining/activities/services information. Nonetheless, we are not far from Montpelier, Vermont (~30 minutes drive) or Burlington, Vermont (~50 minutes drive). Because these cities are a bit larger than Waitsfield, Vermont, you may find larger chain hotels if that is what you are looking for!

Q: What is a Red Envelope aka HongBao?
A: Traditionally, the red envelope (aka hóngbāo 紅包) is a red envelope that is often filled with money, given as a gift during holidays or special occasions, such as weddings. While we have a registry, for those who wish to give a red envelope (aka hóngbāo 紅包), we will also have a box at the reception designated for these red envelopes. Although please do not feel pressured to give or bring a gift - Your presence is truly our most valuable gift!
